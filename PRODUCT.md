# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Consulting candidates preparing for case interviews within the next 1-8 weeks. Primarily final-year undergrads, MBA students, and career switchers targeting MBB (McKinsey, Bain, BCG), Big Four advisory (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG), and boutique strategy firms. They practice alone at any hour — often late at night before an interview — and need structured, strict feedback, not encouragement. Secondary: career switchers (engineers, finance professionals) who are strong analytically but weak in synthesis and consulting-style communication.

## Product Purpose

CaseForge AI helps consulting candidates become interview-ready through realistic company-style mock interviews, strict rubric-based scoring across 7 dimensions (structure, hypothesis, quantitative, business judgment, synthesis, communication, confidence), persistent weakness tracking across sessions, and a dashboard that makes improvement measurable. It sits between free general AI (too agreeable, no memory, no structure) and expensive human coaching (unaffordable at volume). The core loop: mock interview → scorecard with transcript evidence → weakness tags → targeted drill → dashboard progress.

## Brand Personality

Strict, precise, demanding. This is a tough coach, not a cheerleader. The product does not soften feedback. It scores hard, expects more, and makes candidates better. Think: Bloomberg Terminal meets a demanding McKinsey interviewer. Confident expertise, not startup friendliness.

## Anti-references

- ChatGPT / generic AI chat UI (too soft, too agreeable, no structure)
- Duolingo (gamified, playful — wrong emotional register)
- Notion (too editorial, too whitespace-heavy)
- Warm cream/sand/beige SaaS landing pages (the saturated AI default of 2026)
- Card grids with icons and cheerful copy (Canva, Loom style)
- Anything that looks like it "supports" the user rather than challenges them

## Design Principles

1. **Structured severity**: Feedback is hard truth, not gentle suggestion. The UI should reinforce this — precise layouts, no rounding-off of uncomfortable information.
2. **Evidence over assertion**: Every score needs a quote, every claim needs a source. The interface shows the receipts.
3. **Progress as accountability**: The dashboard is not a trophy case — it is a mirror. Show what changed, what didn't, what needs work.
4. **Disappear into the task**: During the interview, the UI vanishes. No chrome, no distraction — just the conversation.
5. **Density earns trust**: Consulting candidates respect precision. Compressed, information-rich interfaces signal competence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Focus states visible. Keyboard navigable. prefers-reduced-motion respected. Color never the sole indicator. Form labels present.
