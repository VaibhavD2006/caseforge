# MVP Scope

## MVP goal

Build the smallest product that proves users will pay for AI-driven consulting interview practice because it gives more rigorous, more personalized, and more measurable improvement than free general AI tools or lightweight mock platforms.

## MVP success criteria

- Users complete at least 3 mock interviews in the first 7 days.
- Users return to review feedback and retake at least 1 weak-skill drill.
- Users can clearly understand why they received a given score.
- Users perceive company-style realism and strictness as better than generic ChatGPT use.
- Users can observe score trends across sessions.

## Must-have features

### 1. Auth and profile

- Email or magic-link sign-in.
- Candidate profile: target firms, interview date, experience level, target role.
- Optional baseline self-assessment.

### 2. Interview modes

- Case interview mock.
- Market sizing mock.
- Behavioral / fit mock.
- Drill mode for targeted practice.

These modes align with the broader market expectation that AI consulting prep tools should support full mocks plus focused drills.[cite:1][cite:2]

### 3. Firm-style templates

- MBB-style interviewer-led case.
- Big Four advisory / strategy style case.
- Boutique strategy style case.
- Generic consulting style fallback.

The market already differentiates around firm-specific or role-specific simulation, so company-style templates are necessary even in MVP.[cite:1][cite:2]

### 4. Scoring engine

Score each session on a 1 to 10 scale for:
- Structure
- Hypothesis quality
- Quantitative accuracy
- Business judgment
- Synthesis
- Communication
- Confidence / conciseness

General AI tools are criticized for inconsistent grading and soft feedback, so a stable rubric-based scoring layer is one of the MVP's most important value drivers.[cite:1][cite:2]

### 5. Feedback report

After each interview:
- Overall score
- Dimension-level scores
- Top 3 strengths
- Top 3 weaknesses
- Exact moments where answers broke down
- Personalized next drills

### 6. Weakness memory

- Persist recurring weakness tags across sessions.
- Show “most repeated issues” on dashboard.
- Use weakness history to generate next best drills.

This directly addresses a recurring limitation identified in both sources: most AI tools do not reliably remember a user's weak spots over time.[cite:1][cite:2]

### 7. Dashboard

- Score history
- Skill trend charts
- Weakness heatmap
- Interview count
- Readiness level
- Rank tier such as Bronze, Silver, Gold, Emerald

### 8. Admin content system

- Internal structured case library.
- Rubric configuration.
- Prompt template management.

## Nice-to-have, but not MVP

- Live voice conversation.
- Speech-to-text and tone analysis.
- Peer leaderboard.
- Coach marketplace.
- Campus admin portal.
- Video answer analysis.
- Full benchmarking against all users.

## Explicit non-goals

- Replacing elite human coaching.
- Full enterprise reporting for universities in version one.
- Deep multimodal evaluation in MVP.
- Perfect simulation of every firm's exact interview style.

## MVP wedge

The MVP should win on four points:
- Stricter feedback than general AI.
- Better weak-spot memory than both general AI and many existing tools.[cite:1][cite:2]
- Firm-style practice rather than one generic interviewer.[cite:1]
- Dashboard-based progress tracking rather than isolated sessions.[cite:1]

## Suggested pricing for MVP testing

- Free: 1 full mock + limited drills.
- Pro monthly: unlimited mocks, dashboard history, weakness tracking.
- Premium later: voice mode, benchmark insights, coach review.

This mirrors existing market norms where tools combine a free entry point with paid ongoing practice access.[cite:1]
