# Ideal Customer Profile

## Product category

AI-native consulting interview preparation SaaS for candidates targeting strategy consulting, Big Four consulting, and adjacent advisory firms.

## Core ICP

The primary customer is a high-intent consulting candidate who needs repeated mock interview practice, objective feedback, and structured skill improvement without relying entirely on peers or expensive coaching.

## Primary segment

### Pre-offer consulting candidate

- Final-year undergraduate, master's student, MBA, or early-career professional.
- Applying to MBB, Big Four, Tier-2 strategy firms, or boutique consulting firms.
- Already knows that case interviews are important, but lacks enough quality reps.
- Wants flexible practice at any hour.
- Needs more than static casebooks and more consistency than ad hoc peer practice.

This segment is attractive because current AI prep articles explicitly position AI tools as a way to provide high-volume, always-available practice, especially when human partners are unavailable.[cite:2] The strongest purpose-built tools are marketed around full mocks, drills, and scoring, which validates that candidates already pay for this workflow.[cite:1]

## Secondary segments

### Big Four and advisory candidate

- Preparing for Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG, Accenture, and strategy-advisory style interviews.
- Needs a mix of case, behavioral, market-sizing, and situational practice.
- Often underserved by MBB-heavy prep brands.

### Career-switching professional

- Comes from engineering, finance, operations, or startup backgrounds.
- Strong analytically, but weaker in synthesis, communication, and consulting-style structuring.
- Values guided improvement more than open-ended practice.

### University club and cohort buyer

- Consulting clubs, placement cells, and bootcamps that want group access.
- Useful later as a B2B2C motion after consumer validation.

## User pains

- Not enough high-quality live case partners.
- Peer practice is inconsistent and often too lenient.
- Human coaching is expensive.
- General AI tools are too agreeable and inconsistent in grading.[cite:1][cite:2]
- Existing platforms do not always remember repeated weaknesses over time.[cite:1][cite:2]
- Users struggle to know whether they are improving or just practicing randomly.[cite:1]

## Desired outcomes

- Practice realistic interviews in company-specific styles.
- Receive strict, repeatable feedback after every session.
- See clear progress across structure, math, synthesis, communication, and business judgment.
- Get targeted drills based on recurring weaknesses.
- Build confidence before real interviews.

## Buying triggers

- Interview invitation received.
- Application season starting in the next 2 to 8 weeks.
- Dissatisfaction with peer practice quality.
- Perception that coaching is too expensive.
- Need for structured solo prep.

## Why they buy this product

Purpose-built consulting tools are already differentiated by full mock cases, scoring rubrics, drill engines, and voice practice, which shows there is real willingness to pay for a more structured prep experience than free general AI can provide.[cite:1][cite:2] A better product wins by making progress measurable and company-specific, not just by offering more chat sessions.[cite:1]

## Positioning statement

For consulting candidates who need rigorous and repeatable interview practice, this product is an AI consulting interview trainer that simulates company-style interviews, scores performance on recruiter rubrics, remembers recurring weaknesses, and converts prep into a measurable readiness system.
